Calculating π using Kepler's third law

Brief description

Today I used the formula a³/T²=G(M+m)/4π² from the Kepler's third law.
Transforming the said equation will yield
π=√(T²G(M+m)/4a³)
where T is the orbital period, G is the gravitational constant, and a is the semi-major axis of the orbit.
